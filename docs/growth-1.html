Chapter 2 Growth 1

Using Stan with a lognormal likelihood.

We used the following growth model with a lognormal distribution to estimate individual growth potential in stan:

\[\begin{equation} DBH_{y=today,s,i} - DBH_{y=y0,s,i} \sim \\ \mathcal{logN} (log(\sum _{y=y_0} ^{y=today} \theta_{1,s}.exp(-\frac12.[\frac{log(\frac{DBH_{y,s,i}}{100.\theta_{2,s}})}{\theta_{3,s}}]^2)), \sigma_1) \\ \theta_{1,s,i} = e^{log(\theta_{1,s}) + \sigma_2.\epsilon_{1,i}} \\ \theta_{2,s} = e^{log(\theta_2) + \sigma_3.\epsilon_{2,s}} \\ \theta_{3,s} = e^{log(\theta_3) + \sigma_4.\epsilon_{3,s}} \\ \epsilon_{1,i} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1) \\ \epsilon_{2,p} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1) \\ \epsilon_{3,p} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1) \\ ~ \\ (\theta_{1,p}, \theta_2, \theta_3) \sim \mathcal{logN}^3(log(1),1) \\ (\sigma_1, \sigma_2, \sigma_3, \sigma_4) \sim \mathcal{N}^4_T(0,1) \\ \tag{2.1} \end{equation}\]
